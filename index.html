About

The manta identification project started in 1991 by Keller Laros, founder of Manta Pacific Research Foundation. Each manta ray has a unique spot pattern on its ventral side. These spot patterns can be used to accurately identify individuals. The first manta ray ever identified was Lefty. She has a broken left cepahlic fin so she is easy to differentiate from the others, but Lefty also has a spot pattern on her abdomen that is unique to her. Manta Pacfic Research Foundation maintains an ID Catalog of mantas in Kona and Hawaii.
